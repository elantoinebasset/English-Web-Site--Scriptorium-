Arabic Writing System

كتابة السلام

How to Recognize It?

Distinctive Features of the Arabic Alphabet
  • Abjad System: Primarily consonant-based; vowels are often omitted or shown with diacritics.
  • Right-to-Left Script: Written and read from right to left.
  • Cursive Nature: Most letters connect to others, changing shape based on position (initial, medial, final, isolated).
  • Diacritical Marks: Used for pronunciation, grammar, and vowel sounds—especially in learning and religious texts.
  • No Capitalization: Arabic script doesn’t use uppercase or lowercase distinctions.
  • Widespread Use: Used across the Arab world and in languages like Persian, Urdu, and Pashto (with slight modifications).

Writing Basics

The Arabic alphabet is a writing system used in many languages across the Middle East, North Africa, and parts of Asia. It is a consonant-based script written from right to left, and its letters change shape depending on their position in a word.

Basic Principles
  • Right to left orientation
  • No distinction between uppercase and lowercase
  • Letters change form based on position (initial, medial, final, isolated)
  • Arabic numerals or Eastern Arabic numerals may be used
  • Diacritics indicate short vowels or pronunciation guides

History

Origins and Evolution

The Arabic script originated in the 4th century CE, evolving from the Nabataean Aramaic script. It became widespread with the rise of Islam in the 7th century and was used to write the Quran, which helped standardize its form and spread it across the Islamic world.

  • 4th century CE: Emergence from Nabataean script
  • 7th century: Standardized with the Quran
  • 10th–13th century: Spread across North Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia
  • Today: Used by 20+ languages and over 400 million people
Fun Facts
  • The Arabic script is cursive, meaning letters connect within words.
  • It has 28 core letters, but additional characters are used in other languages like Persian and Urdu.
  • Arabic calligraphy is a major art form and cultural expression.
  • Arabic script is one of the most widely used writing systems in the world.
