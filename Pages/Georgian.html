Georgian Writing System

მშვიდობის წერა

(Mshvidobis Tsera – "Writing Peace")

How to Recognize It?

Distinctive Features of the Georgian Alphabet
  • Unique Alphabet: Georgian has its own alphabet, unrelated to Latin, Greek, or Cyrillic.
  • Modern Script – Mkhedruli: The most widely used form today, consisting of 33 letters.
  • No Uppercase: Unlike many other scripts, Georgian has no capital letters.
  • Smooth, flowing style: Letters are curvy and artistic, often compared to calligraphy.
  • Written Left to Right: Directionality is the same as Latin scripts.
  • Fixed Letter Set: All 33 letters represent distinct sounds with no diacritics.

Writing Basics

The Georgian script is an alphabetic writing system in which each character corresponds to a specific sound. The current script in daily use is called Mkhedruli, known for its artistic and flowing appearance.

Basic Principles
  • Written left to right, top to bottom
  • 33 letters, each representing a phoneme
  • No uppercase/lowercase distinction
  • No diacritics or special accent marks
  • Punctuation is similar to Latin systems
  • Numbers use standard Arabic numerals (0–9)

History

Origins and Evolution

The Georgian writing system has evolved through three primary scripts: Asomtavruli, Nuskhuri, and Mkhedruli. While all three are still used in religious contexts, Mkhedruli became the secular and eventually the universal script for the Georgian language.

Timeline of Development
  • 5th century: Asomtavruli script appears in early inscriptions
  • 9th century: Nuskhuri script is used in ecclesiastical writing
  • 11th century: Mkhedruli emerges and gains use in royal documents
  • 18th–19th century: Mkhedruli becomes standardized and secular
  • 2016: Georgian writing system recognized by UNESCO as intangible cultural heritage
Fun Facts
  • Georgian is one of only a handful of languages with its own unique writing system.
  • There are no capital letters in modern Georgian writing.
  • The Mkhedruli script is often used in calligraphy and tattoo designs for its visual elegance.
  • All three Georgian scripts are included in the Unicode standard.
  • Georgian is part of the Kartvelian language family, unrelated to Indo-European or Turkic languages.
  • Many letters may look similar to outsiders but have very different sounds and meanings.
